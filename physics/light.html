Important Definitions:

Incident Ray

The ray of light that strikes any surface.

Reflected Ray

The ray of light that bounces back from a reflecting surface.

Point of Incidence

The point where the Incident Ray meets the reflecting surface.

Normal

The line perpendicular to the reflecting surface at the Point of Incidence.

Angle of Incidence

The angle between the Incident Ray and the Normal at the Point of Incidence.

Angle of Reflection

The angle between the Reflected Ray and the Normal at the Point of Incidence.

Reflection Of Light

Object

Anything emitting light or reflecting light.

Point Object

A very small object like a pinhead, represented by a dot.

Extended Object

Large objects like cars or buses, represented by an arrow.

Image

The point where light rays from an object meet or appear to meet after reflection.

Real Image

An image formed by the actual intersection of light rays, like on a cinema screen.

Virtual Image

An image formed when light rays appear to meet but do not actually intersect, like in a plane mirror.

Plane Mirror

A glass sheet with a thin layer of silver metal, reflecting light.

Properties of Light

  • It's a non-mechanical wave, not requiring any medium to travel.
  • Wavelength of light ranges from violet (400nm) to red (700nm).
  • Speed of light in vacuum is approximately 299,792,458 m/s.
  • It's a transverse wave.
  • Light always travels in straight lines (Rectilinear Propagation).

Propagation of Light

  • In a homogeneous medium, light travels in a straight line (Rectilinear Propagation).
  • Light changes its path due to obstacles or change in the medium.

Ray of Light

A straight path of light.

Beam of light

A bundle of rays of light.

Laws of Reflection

  • The angle of incidence is always equal to the angle of reflection.
  • Incident ray, reflected ray, and the normal form a plane.

Kinds of Reflection:

Regular Reflection

When parallel rays of light reflected from a smooth surface are parallel, it's called Regular Reflection. It helps in forming images, like seeing our face in a mirror.

Diffused or Irregular Reflection

When parallel rays reflected from rough surfaces are not parallel, it's called Diffused or Irregular Reflection. It doesn't form clear images.

Reflection at Plane Mirror:

Properties of image formed by a plane mirror

  • Image formed by a plane mirror is virtual.

  • Image formed by a plane mirror is always erect.

  • Size of the image formed by a plane mirror is the same as that of the object.

  • Distance of the image from a plane mirror is equal to the distance of the object from the mirror.

  • Image formed by a plane mirror is laterally inverted.

  • Important Note: Though every part of a mirror forms a complete image of an object, we usually see only the part where light after reflection from the mirror reaches.
