Important Definitions:

Incident Ray

The ray of light which strikes any surface is called the Incident Ray.

Reflected Ray

The ray of light which is bounced back by any reflecting surface is called the Reflected Ray

Point of Incidence

It is the point at which the Incident Ray falls on the reflecting surface

Normal

It is the line drawn perpendicular to the reflecting surface at the Point of Incidence

Angle of Incidence

The angle made by the Incident Ray with the Normal at the Point of Incidence. It is represented by i

Angle of Reflection

Angle made by the Reflected ray with the Normal at the Point of Incidence. It is represented by r

The Reflection Of Light

Object

Anything which gives out light either by itself or after reflection from it is said to be an object. For example: a candle, tubelight,etc.

Point Object

A very small object likr pin head is a point object and is represented by a dot (.)

Extended Object

Large object like car, bus, candle etc. are called extended objects and are represented by an arrow.( ↑)

Image

An image may be defined as that point, where the light rays coming from an object meet or appears to meet after reflection There are two types of images i.e: real image and virtual image.

Real Image

Real image is an image formed by the actual inersection of light rays.

It can be obtained on the screen.

eg:- The image formed on cinema screen

Virtual Image

If the rays of light responsible for an image formation do not intersect actually but appears to meet at some point, then the image is called virtual image.

It cannot be obtained on the screen.

eg:- The image formed by a plane mirror.

Plane Mirror

A plane glass sheet having a thin layer of silver metal deposited on one side which is protected by a coat of red paint. The light is reflected from silvered surface of the plane mirror.

Properties of Light

  • Its is a non-mechanical wave as it dosen't require any medium to travel.
  • Wavelength of light ranges from 400nm[Voilet] to 700nm[Red].
  • Speed of light in vaccum is exactly 299,792,458 m/s or nearly [ 3 x 10 8 m/s]
  • It is Transverse wave.
  • Light always travel in straight line [Rectilinear Propogation].

Propogation of Light

  • In homogenous medium light travls in straight line This called rectilinear propogation of light
  • Light changes its path due to following reason:-
  • Due to some objects or things comes in between the path of the light.
  • Due to change in medium.

Ray of Light

A straight line path of light is called ray of light.

Beam of light

A bundle of ray of light is called Beam of light.

Kinds of Reflection:

Regular Reflection

When all prallel rays of light falls on reflected from a plane smooth surface are prallel, the reflection is known as Regular Reflection. It helps in the formation of image and hence we can see our face in mirror.

Diffused or Irregular Reflection

When all prallel rays reflected from a rough or uneven surfaces are not prallel, the reflection is known as Diffused or Irregular Reflection. Diffused Reflection does not give rise to image formation.

Properties of image formed by plane mirror

  • Image formed by plane mirror is virtual

  • Image formed by plane mirror is always erect

  • Size of image formed by plane mirror is same as that of object.

  • Distance of image from plane mirror is equal to the distance of object from mirror.

  • Image formed by plane mirror is laterally inverted.

  • Though every part of a mirror froms a complete image of an object, we usually see that part of it which light after reflection from mirror reaches on it.
